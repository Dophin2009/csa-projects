II. Research Question

Question: For a list in reverse order, is merge sort or quicksort faster?
Hypothesis: When sorting a list in reverse order, quicksort will be faster than merge sort.
Conclusion: After running the tests and plotting the data (in the third chart), it is very evident, even with small lists, that for lists already sorted in reverse order, merge sort is much faster than quicksort.
Even with the manual increase of node's maximum call stack size, a RangeError was encountered due to the recursion of quicksort for lists longer than ~7000 elements.
For all list sizes tested, merge sort maintained a run-time under 1 millisecond, whereas quicksort's run-times increased greatly.
In conclusion, my hypothesis was incorrect. Contrary to my prediction, merge sort was much faster than quicksort on lists in revevrse order.
